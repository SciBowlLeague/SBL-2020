About

The (unofficial) National Science Bowl Discord server is proud to announce the first-ever online Science Bowl League! Students will be given the opportunity to compete with teams across the country in a fast-paced buzzer competition in math and science. The league-style showdown will occur over 6 weeks, and the top 8 teams at the end of the regular season will advance to the single elimination Championship Tournament. The finals will consist of a best-of-three series that will be broadcast live.

The registration deadline is September 20th, the payment deadline is October 4th, and the first matches are on October 11th. Matches will be played over Discord in a manner similar to the Science Bowl Spring Tournament (SBST). The competition will follow official NSB rules for scoring, timekeeping, etc. modified for an online format. If you have any questions regarding the League, check out the FAQ section. If it's unresolved, feel free to ask in the SBL Discord or directly to the staff through email.

FAQs

This year’s National Finals were unique in that there was no buzzing and conferring on tossups was allowed. SBL will instead follow in-person rules — we will be emulating real buzzing, and we will not allow conferring on tossups. All timing and score keeping will be in accordance to official NSB rules. Check out the SBST YouTube videos for more info.

Our writing team is comprised entirely of regional champions, several of which have achieved national and international recognition in Olympiads. Our Staff also has plenty of experience running Science Bowl tournaments, including SBST, the Connecticut Science Bowl League (CSBL), and the Lexington Online Summer Tournament (LOST).

We currently expect between 16 and 32 teams. This is not a guarantee — SBST had 36 participating teams, so we are prepared to accommodate as many teams as we can.

Depending on the number of participating teams, we may choose to categorize teams based on their skill level to provide an enjoyable environment for everyone. In this scenario, we will likely have two divisions, one for more competitive teams and another for more casual teams.

This is highly dependent on the composition of teams. If most participants are Nationals- level, for example, we will likely have more difficult questions. If we have divisions, the questions may vary depending on which division you are in. We can guarantee that we will provide challenging questions for all participants.

We will have several outlets for players to anonymously report others suspected of cheating. We are also exploring the option of requiring players to record their immediate surroundings prior to each match, much like was done this year by NSB®. We take cheating very seriously and are committed to providing a fair environment to everyone.

We can confirm we will be streaming the finals of the Championship tournament on YouTube or Twitch. We will also likely stream some select league matches, with proper precautions to prevent cheating.

Currently, we only allow 5 people per team, with only 4 playing at a given time (as per official NSB rules). We will give the option to have one or two last-minute backup players for any extenuating circumstances.

We will allow teams to apply for a ‘B’ team at a reduced cost. NOTE: ‘A’ teams will be given precedence over ‘B’ teams, so a ‘B’ team will never take the spot of an ‘A’ team wishing to compete.

Every week, there will be specified days when matches can be completed. On these days, there will be several time “slots” when the SBL Staff is available. Teams will agree on a time and sign up together for a slot in advance. If none of the time slots work for both teams, and there are no backup players available, the Staff will organize a different time for teams to compete.
